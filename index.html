About

Pangea is a group of student committed to helping our fellow students, teachers, parents, etc. find the best and appropriate school for themselves or others. We hope that you find this information helpful.

We dedicated most of our last school semester gathering data from resources like NICHE and surveys we created for student thoughts on their school. We ensure that our data from all the school are recent to the best of our ability.

College Readness vs Graduation Rate

The color gradient shows the graduation rate, the darker the higher the percentage. The size of the bubble shows the level of college readiness that the school provides. With this, we see that the Herron, Lawrence North, and North Central are the top three school with regards to college readiness followed by Cathedral, Highlands Highschool, and Pike High School in the following three spots. Now within these six schools, Herron is still top three but now Highlands High and Pike take the other two spots and push Cathedral to the lowest. There seems to be a slight correlation between these topics but there are still some missing links.

Diversity vs Free or Reduced Lunch

This graph tends to speak for its self. The color of the bubbles represents the about of students on free or reduced lunch. The size expresses the degree of diversity in that type of schools. There seems to be a clear correlation between diversity and the number of students on free or reduced lunch. There are only two schools who's diversity are on similar levels, Public, Carter schools and Private, Catholic schools yet the Private, Catholic have a higher number of students on free or reduced lunch.

AP Enrollment vs Student Count

Here we see the number of students in the school, the percentage of students in AP classes and the student to teacher ratio. The darker the color the more students, as we see North Central, Pike High School, and Ben Davis High School have the most number of students in their schools. We can also see that Herron has a similar percentage of students in AP classes than Ben Davis but has much fewer students in their school. Personally, we believe that having a small student count and a large percentage of students in AP classes is best for a school.
